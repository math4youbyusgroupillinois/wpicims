Location and Directions


City and Its Area


Bled is a town in northwestern Slovenia, on the east side of Lake Bled. The area is a popular tourist destination due to its impressive and refreshing scenery; it is also known as Slovenia's Alpine Pearl. The lake is 35km (22mi) from Ljubljana International Airport and 55km (34mi) from the capital city, Ljubljana.

The recommended online for general and specific information (how to get to Bled and how to get around, list of local attractions (what to see and what to do in the area), places to eat and more) is:

  • The Bled page at Wikitravel.org

Visas

Citizens of all European countries, as well as Australia, Canada, Israel, Japan, New Zealand and the United States do not require visas to enter Slovenia for stays of up to 90 days.
Any prospective participants who need visas to attend the meeting are kindly asked to contact the Seminar Secretary, Vesna Nahtigal, providing the following information:

  • Family name and given name
  • Sex (male or female)
  • Date of birth
  • Nationality
  • Passport number
  • Professional specialty
  • Occupation & title / position
  • Organization/institution and department, including postal address and postcode
  • Address for correspondence, including postcode
An invitation letter will be sent to individual visa applicants by e-mail.
